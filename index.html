Mistakes

The best way to visualize data efficiently is probably to avoid the most common mistakes.

From data to viz offers you a gallery of common caveats

Order your data!

When the display the value of several entities, ordering it makes it way more insightful.

To cut or not to cut?

Cuting the Y axis is one of the most controversial practice in data viz. Indeed, it can make the plot more insightful.. or allow to lie with data.

A bit of spaghetti

A line graph with too many lines becomes unreadable: it is called a spaghetti graph.

Pie chart

Probably the easiest mistake to avoid: just don't do it.

Histogram

Very close from a density. Cut the data in several bins and show the number of entry per bin.

Ridgeline

Allows to compare the distribution of a few variables. Usefull if there is a pattern to observe

Density

Show the density of a numeric variable

About

A density plot shows the distribution of a numeric variable. It takes only numeric variables as input and is very close from an histogram. It can be use in the same exact condition.

Common Mistakes
  • Play with the bandwith of your density function.
  • Don't show the distribution of more than ~5 variables. Use Violin or Ridge line plot instead.
